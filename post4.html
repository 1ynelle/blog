Post 4 - Portfolio Research

November 18, 2020

I think a good design portfolio site is one that shows the designer's personality and a few of their best projects.

Sites I've visited have a simple navigation - home, about, contact, work - where a visitor can easily scroll through and get a sense of the type of work the designer is good at, the type of work they want to do, and the type of designer they are through a mix of real work and personal projects.

There are minimal words - usually a paragraph or two about what the project was for, the process they follow, and the outcome. Finally, buttons and previews of other projects encourage visitors to view more of the designer's work or take them back to the home screen.
