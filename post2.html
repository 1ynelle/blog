Post 2 - Article Process

November 5, 2020

Since there weren't many elements to style in my article, I tried to keep the layout simple, separating the header from the main content. After the header title and image, I used sections to divide Harjo's questions from MLK's.

The article on semantic HTML tags clarified the purpose and placement of these tags in HTML. Here are my main takeaways:

  • Semantic tags help with SEO and those with visual impairments.
  • A section (generally) forms a part of something else, while an article can stand on its own. More important is that the structure is logical and consistent.
  • A tag outside a tag (like an aside outside an article tag) shows they're indirectly related

Similar to my article's structure, I tried to keep the styling minimal and straightforward. I wanted visitors to focus on the text, so I placed more emphasis on titles, subheadings, and quotes. I'm trying to experiment with more colors and details, but contrast issues and making sure everything is communicating what I hope to communicate are my greatest difficulties when it comes to visual design.
